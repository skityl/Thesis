\abstract{\addtocontents{toc}{\vspace{1em}} % Add a gap in the Contents, for aesthetics

The quantised calculus is a tool originating from non-commutative geometry
that provides a rigorous ``calculus of infinitesimals". We give an
in depth study of the quantised calculus in two situations: on the real
line and on the circle. 


%In his 1994 book \emph{Non-commutative geometry} Alain Connes introduced
%the quantised calculus. This is a means of doing calculus which makes sense
%in a high degree of generality, and has many properties analogous to
%the classical notion of ``infinitesimal calculus". This thesis
%is an in-depth study of quantised calculus in two cases: on the real line
%and on the circle.

}
